These factors cause reduction in power factor. Thus two or more component factors are taken into account for power factor improvement by neglecting the input of other components. The boost converter is used to get increased voltage at the output end. If load is varied, the output voltage varies, which causes a change in the input power factor of the circuit. In a PFC, the controller works to improve the input side power factor by adjusting the duty cycle. Here the duty cycle is adjusted for power factor correction in addition to its variation for maintaining constant voltage at the output end. To improve the power factor, the input current is made to follow the input side voltage.
